# 4.0 Scripts: Approved Talk Tracks

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Word Tracks • Discovery</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Ask better questions so the right solution becomes obvious.</div>
  <p>Use these word tracks to keep discovery natural, helpful, and connected to what matters most to the guest.</p>
</div>

<div class="do-dont">
  <div class="dont-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Avoid</span>
    <p>“What payment do you want?” before understanding needs.</p>
    <p>Showing random vehicles before clarifying priorities.</p>
    <p>Delaying appraisal until the end and creating surprises.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="do-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Use</span>
    <p>Open questions, reflective summaries, early appraisal language, and a clean bridge to 2–3 best-fit options.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Discovery word tracks
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Discovery Opening</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Welcome in — before we look at anything, I want to make sure we narrow this down fast. What brought you in today, and what’s most important in your next vehicle?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Usage + Lifestyle</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Walk me through a normal week — commute, family, weather, trips. What does the vehicle need to do, not just be?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Must-Haves</span>
    <div class="script-line">“If we get everything else right, what are the 2–3 things you must have?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“And what’s a deal-breaker so we don’t waste your time?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Budget Comfort</span>
    <div class="script-line">“To keep this simple — do you have a payment comfort zone you’d like to stay near?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Totally fine if you’re not sure — we can use a range.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Early Appraisal</span>
    <div class="script-line">“If you have a trade, I’d like to start a quick appraisal now. It saves time and prevents surprises later — sound fair?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Appraisal Transparency</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Our goal is accuracy and fairness. We’ll look at condition, history, and real market data. If you have questions as we go, I’ll walk through it with you.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">No Trade Transition</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Perfect — then we can focus fully on matching the right vehicle to your needs and budget.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Service Customer Discovery</span>
    <div class="script-line">“How’s your vehicle been treating you? Still fitting your life, or have you been thinking about upgrading?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“While you’re here, would a quick trade appraisal be helpful just to know your options?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Transition to selection
<div class="training-card dark">
  <span class="label-pill">Core Bridge</span>
  <p class="muted">Use this before showing vehicles.</p>
  <div class="script-line">“Let me recap to make sure I got it: you need (A), (B), and (C), and you’d like to stay around (budget/comfort). I have 2–3 options that fit — let’s look at them together.”</div>
</div>

---

## Manager coaching note
<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Listen for this:</strong> The salesperson should not sound like they are interviewing the guest. They should sound like they are helping the guest save time and make a better decision.</p>
</div>
