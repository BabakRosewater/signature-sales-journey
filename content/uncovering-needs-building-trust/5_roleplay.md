# 5.0 Role-Play: Discovery + Trust

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Practice Reps</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Turn discovery into a natural conversation, not an interview.</div>
  <p>Pair up as Customer / Consultant. Run each scenario for 6 minutes, switch roles, then score with the rubric.</p>
</div>

<div class="score-row">
  <div class="score-card">0 = Missed</div>
  <div class="score-card">1 = Partial</div>
  <div class="score-card">2 = Clear</div>
</div>

---

## Scenario cards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Scenario 1 • Family + Safety</span>
    <p><strong>Customer:</strong> Needs a family vehicle, unsure on model, concerned about safety and comfort.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Uncover must-haves, timeline, and budget comfort zone.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Walk me through a normal week — commute, family, weather, trips. What does the vehicle need to do, not just be?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Scenario 2 • Towing + Daily Driving</span>
    <p><strong>Customer:</strong> Wants towing capability but worries about size and daily comfort.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Clarify towing realities, daily usage, and 2–3 right-fit directions.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“When you say towing, what are you towing, how often, and how far?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Scenario 3 • Trade Distrust</span>
    <p><strong>Customer:</strong> Has a trade and expects the dealership to lowball them.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Introduce appraisal early with transparency and bring manager support if needed.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“If you have a trade, I’d like to start a quick appraisal now. It saves time and prevents surprises later — sound fair?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Scenario 4 • No Trade + Budget Focus</span>
    <p><strong>Customer:</strong> Sold privately and wants best value with a clean process.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Keep momentum and do not treat no-trade as less serious.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Perfect — then we can focus fully on matching the right vehicle to your needs and budget.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Scenario 5 • Service Customer</span>
    <p><strong>Customer:</strong> Waiting in service and casually mentions their current vehicle may not fit anymore.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Explore future needs without pressure.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“How’s your vehicle been treating you? Still fitting your life, or have you been thinking about upgrading?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Scenario 6 • Jumping Too Fast</span>
    <p><strong>Customer:</strong> Says they want a Tucson, but the salesperson has not clarified why.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Slow down and uncover the reason behind the vehicle choice.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Before we look at that one, what drew you to the Tucson specifically?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Role-play scorecard
<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Score 0–2 for each area. Max score: 12.</strong></p>
  <ol>
    <li>Open-ended questions first</li>
    <li>Active listening and summarizing</li>
    <li>Captured must-haves and deal-breakers</li>
    <li>Clarified budget or payment comfort</li>
    <li>Handled trade / no-trade correctly</li>
    <li>Transition to selection was confident and customer-focused</li>
  </ol>
</div>

## Coaching debrief
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Ask After Each Drill</span>
    <ol>
      <li>Did the salesperson understand the guest’s real use case?</li>
      <li>Did they ask or assume?</li>
      <li>Did they summarize clearly?</li>
      <li>Did appraisal feel like a benefit?</li>
      <li>Did the transition to selection feel earned?</li>
    </ol>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Watch Points</span>
    <ul>
      <li>Too many yes/no questions</li>
      <li>Jumping to inventory too fast</li>
      <li>Avoiding budget comfort</li>
      <li>Delaying appraisal</li>
      <li>Not writing down guest priorities</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
