# 3.0 Success Standards

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Non-Negotiables</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Decision before numbers. Value before payment. Clarity before commitment.</div>
  <p>These standards keep the Decision Point calm, respectful, and trust-first.</p>
</div>

<div class="quick-grid four">
  <div class="training-card blue"><span class="label-pill">Fit</span><p>Guest understands why the vehicle fits their life.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Value</span><p>Benefits are connected to outcomes and feelings.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Commitment</span><p>Use micro-commitments, not pressure closes.</p></div>

</div>

## Seven standards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 1</span>
    <p><strong>Decision before numbers.</strong></p>
    <p>Before payments, the guest should clearly understand why it fits and what value it creates in real life.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 2</span>
    <p><strong>2–3 options max.</strong></p>
    <p>More options create anxiety. We protect clarity by narrowing choices.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 3</span>
    <p><strong>Use their words.</strong></p>
    <p>Summarize priorities in the guest’s language. Guests trust what feels remembered.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“You said safety and winter confidence matter most because of your commute.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 4</span>
    <p><strong>Two-layer value: Feature → Outcome → Feeling.</strong></p>
    <p>Every key point should answer what it does for them and how it makes life feel.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“This feature gives you confidence when the roads get messy.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 5</span>
    <p><strong>Micro-commitment only.</strong></p>
    <p>We do not trap guests. We invite clarity and next steps.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“If we can make the numbers comfortable, would you feel good moving forward with this one?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 6</span>
    <p><strong>Numbers transition equals service step.</strong></p>
    <p>Numbers are information, not confrontation.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Would it be helpful if we looked at what this breaks down to with the options you chose?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 7</span>
    <p><strong>Calm tone always.</strong></p>
    <p>The Decision Point should feel clear, respectful, confident, and simple.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Manager Watch Point</span>
    <p>If the salesperson jumps to numbers before confirming fit and value, coach them back to the Decision Point flow.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Green / Yellow / Red standard
<div class="quick-grid three">
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Green</span><p>Guest can explain why the vehicle fits and is ready to see numbers.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Yellow</span><p>Guest likes the vehicle but fit, value, or confidence is still unclear.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card red"><span class="label-pill">Red</span><p>Salesperson jumps to payment before the guest understands value.</p></div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Manager inspection question:</strong> Did the guest understand the value before we asked them to look at numbers?</p>
</div>
