# 5.0 Role-Play Lab: Decision Point & Building Value

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Practice Reps</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Practice making the next step feel earned, not forced.</div>
  <p>Use groups of three: consultant, guest, and observer/coach. Rotate every 8 minutes.</p>
</div>

<div class="score-row">
  <div class="score-card">1 = Pressure</div>
  <div class="score-card">3 = Clear</div>
  <div class="score-card">5 = Trust-First</div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Score each drill on:</strong> recall of guest priorities, clarity, value-building, calm tone, and next-step transition.</p>
</div>

---

## Drill cards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 1 • 15-Second Recap</span>
    <p><strong>Guest prompt:</strong> “I want something safe and comfortable, but I’m overwhelmed.”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Recap priorities in the guest’s words and add one fit sentence.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“From what you told me, the big priorities are safety, comfort, and keeping this simple — mostly because you want something that feels easy for the family.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 2 • Value Bridge Reps</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Deliver five value bridges without sounding scripted.</p>
    <p><strong>Pick needs:</strong> safety, winter confidence, comfort, tech convenience, space.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Because you mentioned {need}, this {feature} helps give you {outcome/feeling}, especially when {real-life moment}.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 3 • Decision Point Question</span>
    <p><strong>Guest says:</strong> “I like it… I’m just not sure.”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Validate, simplify to two options if needed, and ask one Decision Point question.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Totally fair. Which feels closer to your life — Option A or Option B?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 4 • I Need To Think</span>
    <p><strong>Guest says:</strong> “I need to think about it.”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Validate, isolate uncertainty, clarify, and propose a simple next step.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Totally fair — this is a big decision. Before you think, what part feels unclear or uncertain right now?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 5 • Transition to Numbers</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Make numbers feel like a helpful service step.</p>
    <p><strong>Rules:</strong> No pressure cues. No “close” energy. Just clarity.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Would it be helpful if we looked at what this breaks down to with the options you chose — just so you can see it clearly?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 6 • Partner Alignment</span>
    <p><strong>Scenario:</strong> Guest likes the vehicle but says their partner needs to see it.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Respect the partner while clarifying what the partner will care about most.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Is there anything your partner will care about most — safety, payment, space, or something else — so we can make sure this checks that box too?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Coaching debrief
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Ask After Each Drill</span>
    <ol>
      <li>Did the salesperson use the guest’s words?</li>
      <li>Was the value tied to an outcome, not a spec?</li>
      <li>Did the tone stay calm and respectful?</li>
      <li>Did the next step feel helpful?</li>
      <li>Was there any pressure language?</li>
    </ol>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Watch Points</span>
    <ul>
      <li>Jumping to numbers too early</li>
      <li>Forgetting the guest’s why</li>
      <li>Using a close before value is clear</li>
      <li>Making “I need to think” feel wrong</li>
      <li>Not isolating uncertainty</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
