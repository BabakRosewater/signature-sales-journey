# 5.0 Role-Play Lab: Post-Sale Follow-Up & Loyalty

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Practice Reps</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Practice follow-up that sounds personal, useful, and earned.</div>
  <p>Use groups of three: consultant, customer, and observer/coach. Rotate every 8 minutes.</p>
</div>

<div class="score-row">
  <div class="score-card">1 = Generic</div>
  <div class="score-card">3 = Helpful</div>
  <div class="score-card">5 = Personal + Purposeful</div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Score each drill on:</strong> human tone, helpful reason, personalization, problem ownership, service confidence, and clear next step.</p>
</div>

---

## Drill cards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 1 • 24–48 Hour Help Call</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Warm tone, love + friction question, and one specific help option.</p>
    <p><strong>Guest responses:</strong> “I love it,” “I’m confused by the tech,” or “I’m annoyed by a setting.”</p>
    <div class="script-line">“What’s one thing you love so far, and is there anything that feels confusing or annoying that I can help with?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 2 • 30-Day Service Intro</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Frame service as support, ask permission to schedule, and explain what to expect.</p>
    <p><strong>Guest response:</strong> “I’ll do it later” or “I don’t like service departments.”</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Would you like me to help you get your first appointment set up and tell you exactly what to expect?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 3 • Problem Ownership</span>
    <p><strong>Guest says:</strong> “Nobody called me back about ___.”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Acknowledge, own the next step, promise update time, and follow through.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Thank you for telling me. That makes sense, and I’m going to help you get this handled. Here’s what I’m going to do next: {next step}. I’ll update you by {time/day}.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 4 • Review Ask</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Ask after value is delivered. Keep it short and no-pressure.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“If you feel like we took good care of you, would you be open to leaving a quick review? It helps our team a lot, and I’d really appreciate it.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 5 • Referral Ask</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Make it casual, respectful, and connected to trust.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“If anyone in your circle mentions they’re thinking about a vehicle, feel free to send them my way. I’ll take care of them the same way.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 6 • 90-Day Loyalty Touch</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Sound human and ownership-focused, not salesy.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“How’s the {Vehicle} treating you now that you’ve had some time with it? Anything you want to improve about the ownership experience?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Coaching debrief
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Ask After Each Drill</span>
    <ol>
      <li>Did the follow-up have a helpful purpose?</li>
      <li>Did it reference something personal?</li>
      <li>Did the customer feel supported?</li>
      <li>Was there only one ask?</li>
      <li>Was the next step clear?</li>
    </ol>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Watch Points</span>
    <ul>
      <li>Generic “just checking in” language</li>
      <li>No specific help offered</li>
      <li>Stacking multiple asks</li>
      <li>Not owning a problem</li>
      <li>No CRM follow-up note</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
