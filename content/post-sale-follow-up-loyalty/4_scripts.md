# 4.0 Scripts & Word Tracks

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Word Tracks • Loyalty</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Follow up to help, not to sound like you are checking a box.</div>
  <p>Use these word tracks to support early ownership, introduce service, handle problems, and earn reviews or referrals naturally.</p>
</div>

<div class="do-dont">
  <div class="dont-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Avoid</span>
    <p>“Just checking in,” generic mass-text language, stacked asks, or only calling when you need a review.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="do-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Use</span>
    <p>Helpful purpose, personal detail, ownership support, service confidence, and one clear next step.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Follow-up cadence word tracks
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">24–48 Hour Follow-Up</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Hey {Name}, it’s {Your Name} from Clark Hyundai. Quick one — I wanted to make sure your first day or two with the {Vehicle} has been smooth. What’s one thing you love so far, and is there anything that feels confusing or annoying that I can help with?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">7-Day Follow-Up</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Now that you’ve had a full week, how’s it fitting your day-to-day? Anything you’d like me to walk you through — settings, phone, safety tech, or anything else?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">30-Day Service Intro</span>
    <div class="script-line">“You’re about a month in now — how are things going overall? Also, I want to make your first service visit easy. Would you like me to help you get your first appointment set up and tell you exactly what to expect?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">90-Day Loyalty Touch</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Hey {Name} — quick hello. How’s the {Vehicle} treating you now that you’ve had some time with it? Anything you want to improve about the ownership experience?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Problem handling
<div class="training-card red">
  <span class="label-pill">Own + Solve</span>
  <div class="script-line">“Thank you for telling me. That makes sense, and I’m going to help you get this handled. Here’s what I’m going to do next: {next step}. I’ll update you by {time/day} so you’re not left guessing.”</div>
</div>

---

## Earned asks
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Review Ask</span>
    <div class="script-line">“If you feel like we took good care of you, would you be open to leaving a quick review? It helps our team a lot, and I’d really appreciate it.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Referral Ask</span>
    <div class="script-line">“If anyone in your circle mentions they’re thinking about a vehicle, feel free to send them my way. I’ll take care of them the same way.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Re-engage without pressure
<div class="training-card amber">
  <span class="label-pill">If They Mention Someone Shopping</span>
  <div class="script-line">“No pressure at all — if they want options, I can make it simple and send a couple best-fit ideas.”</div>
</div>

---

## Manager coaching note
<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Listen for this:</strong> Follow-up should sound like ownership support. If it sounds like a sales task, rewrite it around one helpful reason for contact.</p>
</div>
