# 3.0 Success Standards

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Non-Negotiables</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Every follow-up must feel helpful, personal, and purposeful.</div>
  <p>These standards protect ownership confidence and turn delivery into loyalty.</p>
</div>

<div class="quick-grid four">
  <div class="training-card blue"><span class="label-pill">Cadence</span><p>24–48 hours, 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, and service milestones.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Purpose</span><p>Help first. Avoid empty “just checking in.”</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Personal</span><p>Use real notes from the relationship.</p></div>

</div>

## Seven standards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 1</span>
    <p><strong>Minimum follow-up cadence.</strong></p>
    <ul>
      <li>24–48 hours after delivery</li>
      <li>7 days after delivery</li>
      <li>30 days after delivery</li>
      <li>90 days after delivery</li>
      <li>Service milestone touches</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 2</span>
    <p><strong>Follow-up purpose equals help.</strong></p>
    <p>Every contact must answer questions, help with features, confirm satisfaction, introduce service support, or handle an open item.</p>
    <p><strong>Avoid:</strong> empty “just checking in.”</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 3</span>
    <p><strong>Use notes to personalize.</strong></p>
    <p>Reference their priorities, family/lifestyle, trade story, commute, weekend plans, or what they were excited about.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 4</span>
    <p><strong>One ask per touch max.</strong></p>
    <p>Do not stack review, referral, service, accessories, and survey asks in one contact. Choose one based on timing and value delivered.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 5</span>
    <p><strong>If there is a problem, own it and solve it.</strong></p>
    <p>No blame. Acknowledge, take ownership of the next step, and update them until resolved.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Here’s what I’m going to do next, and I’ll update you by {time/day}.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 6</span>
    <p><strong>Service intro must be warm.</strong></p>
    <p>Service should feel like a benefit, support team, and relationship — not a separate building.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 7</span>
    <p><strong>CRM discipline.</strong></p>
    <p>Every follow-up must log outcome, next action, open loops, and service appointment status.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Manager Watch Point</span>
    <p>If follow-up sounds generic, the customer will feel processed instead of remembered.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Green / Yellow / Red standard
<div class="quick-grid three">
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Green</span><p>Follow-up is timely, personal, helpful, logged, and has a clear next step.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Yellow</span><p>Follow-up happened, but it was generic, late, or missing documentation.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card red"><span class="label-pill">Red</span><p>No follow-up, unresolved issue, no service intro, or no CRM record.</p></div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Manager inspection question:</strong> Did this contact make ownership easier for the customer?</p>
</div>
