## Five loyalty principles

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">1 • First 7–10 Days Shape Emotional Memory</span>
    <p>Customers form their strongest ownership impression early. If they feel unsupported, they may reinterpret the purchase negatively.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> follow up quickly, solve friction fast, and reinforce why the vehicle fits their life.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">2 • Helpfulness Builds Loyalty Faster Than Discounts</span>
    <p>People remember support and reliability more than a single good deal.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> offer small wins: setup help, quick answers, service confidence, and ownership tips.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">3 • Consistency Signals Professionalism</span>
    <p>A consistent cadence makes customers feel remembered, not hunted.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> 24–48 hours, 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, and service milestones.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">4 • Reciprocity: Earn the Ask</span>
    <p>When you help first, customers are more open to helping back.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> ask for reviews and referrals after you have delivered support or solved something real.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">5 • Service Familiarity Drives Retention</span>
    <p>Service retention improves when customers know who to contact, what to expect, and feel comfortable walking in.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> first maintenance plan, first appointment setup, and what-to-expect explanation.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Lens</span>
    <p>The strongest loyalty touch is not a sales message. It is a useful ownership-support message.</p>
  </div>
</div>
