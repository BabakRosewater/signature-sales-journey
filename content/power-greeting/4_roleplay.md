# Role-Play Practice: Power Greetings

## Components of a Great Greeting
- Smile  
- Eye contact  
- posture  
- open body language  
- upbeat (not pushy) tone  
- clear cadence

---

## Scenario 1: Welcome to the Showroom

**Consultant:** “Good afternoon — welcome in. What brings you in today?”  
**Guest:** “I’m here for service.”  
**Consultant:** “Absolutely — I’ll walk you right over.” *(escort)*  
**Guest:** “Actually… I’m interested in sales.”  
**Consultant:** “Perfect. I’m ____________. I’m here to help. What are you hoping to accomplish today?”  
**Guest:** “I’m just looking.”  
**Consultant:** “No problem at all. Take your time — and if you want pricing or details on anything, I’ll be right here.”

**Coach Notes**
- confirm purpose first  
- remove pressure  
- give the guest control

---

## Scenario 2: Spotting a Browser

**Consultant:** “Hi — has anyone greeted you yet?”  
**Guest:** “No.”  
**Consultant:** “Welcome. I’m ____________. Please make yourself at home.”  
**Guest:** “I’m just looking.”  
**Consultant:** “Perfect — take your time. If you want features or pricing on any vehicle, I’ll help whenever you’re ready.”

---

## Scenario 3: Drive-Up Inquiry

**Guest:** “Do you have any __________?”  
**Consultant:** “Great question. Pull into customer parking and I’ll check that right now.”  
**Consultant:** “How long have you been considering one?”  
**Guest:** “A while.”  
**Consultant:** “Great. I’m ____________. Is this your first visit with us?”

---

## Scenario 4: Close Proximity (Service)

**Consultant:** “Are we getting you taken care of today?”  
**Guest:** “Yes — service.”  
**Consultant:** “Perfect. How has your __________ been treating you?”  
**Consultant:** “While you’re waiting, if you’re curious, I can show you what’s new. No pressure.”

---

## Scenario 5: Weather-Adapted Welcome

**Consultant:** “Welcome — it’s [cold/hot/wet] out there. Let’s get you comfortable inside. Water or coffee?”  
**Consultant:** “What would you like to accomplish today — shopping or just starting research?”
