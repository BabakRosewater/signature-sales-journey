# 4.0 Scripts & Word Tracks

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Word Tracks • Closing</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Use calm, clear language that makes commitment feel natural.</div>
  <p>These word tracks help salespeople transition into numbers, present options simply, isolate concerns, and close with professionalism.</p>
</div>

<div class="do-dont">
  <div class="dont-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Avoid</span>
    <p>Pressure phrases, over-explaining numbers, filling silence, or asking for commitment before alignment is clear.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="do-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Use</span>
    <p>Recap, simple numbers, alignment check, respectful commitment ask, and clean next steps.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Transition into numbers
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">After Tour</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Now that you’ve seen how we support you, I’ll keep the numbers simple and clear. Sound good?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">30-Second Recap</span>
    <div class="script-line">“From what you told me, the big priorities are {Priority 1}, {Priority 2}, and {Priority 3} — especially because {why}. Did I get that right?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Numbers Setup</span>
    <div class="script-line">“I’m going to show you one best-fit option first. If we need to adjust, we’ll do it together.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Confirm Alignment</span>
    <div class="script-line">“How close is this to what you had in mind?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Commitment and choice
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Commitment Ask</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Would you like to move forward with this one?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Two-Option Close</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Totally fair. We have two clean paths: Option A protects the payment. Option B builds equity faster. Which fits your life better?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Common closing stalls
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">I Need To Think</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Of course. Before you go, what specifically would you like to think through? Is it the vehicle, the payment, or just timing?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“If we could make that one piece feel comfortable, would you be ready to move forward?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">I Want To Shop Around</span>
    <div class="script-line">“That makes sense — this is a big decision. Most people compare two things: fit and numbers. Is there anything about the fit that feels uncertain, or is it mainly the numbers?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Let’s make sure you’re comparing apples to apples. What would you like to use as your must-have baseline?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Payment Too High</span>
    <div class="script-line">“I hear you. Let’s solve it cleanly. Is it the monthly number itself, or how it fits your budget priorities?”</div>
    <p class="muted">Then offer two paths: adjust term/down/payment/vehicle, or alternate selection if needed.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Partner / Spouse</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Absolutely — totally normal. What’s the most important part for them: payment, safety, or overall value? If you’d like, we can send a simple summary so it’s easy to review together.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Clean next step if not today
<div class="training-card dark">
  <span class="label-pill">Trust Intact</span>
  <div class="script-line">“I respect that. Let me summarize where we landed, what still needs to be clear, and the best next step so this stays easy for you.”</div>
</div>

---

## Manager coaching note
<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Listen for this:</strong> The salesperson should not rush, fill silence, or turn every concern into a negotiation. They should clarify the real issue and keep the next step clean.</p>
</div>
