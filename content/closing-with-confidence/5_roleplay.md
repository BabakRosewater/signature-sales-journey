# 5.0 Role-Play Lab: Closing with Confidence

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Practice Reps</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Practice presenting numbers without creating pressure.</div>
  <p>Use groups of three: consultant, guest, and observer/coach. Rotate every 7–8 minutes.</p>
</div>

<div class="score-row">
  <div class="score-card">1 = Pressure</div>
  <div class="score-card">3 = Clear</div>
  <div class="score-card">5 = Calm Confidence</div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Score each drill on:</strong> recap quality, simplicity, calm tone, strategic silence, concern isolation, and next-step clarity.</p>
</div>

---

## Drill cards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 1 • Recap → Numbers → Confirm</span>
    <p><strong>Guest prompt:</strong> “Okay… so what are the numbers?”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> 30-second recap, numbers setup line, and alignment check.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“From what you told me, the big priorities are {P1}, {P2}, and {P3}. I’m going to show you one best-fit option first.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 2 • Strategic Silence</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Present numbers in 20–30 seconds, then pause.</p>
    <p><strong>Observer checks:</strong> Did the consultant fill silence? Did the guest speak first?</p>
    <div class="script-line">“I’ll pause there for a second. What questions come up first?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 3 • Two-Option Close</span>
    <p><strong>Guest prompt:</strong> “I’m not sure.”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Validate, offer two clean options, and ask preference.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Totally fair. We have two clean paths: Option A protects payment. Option B builds equity faster. Which fits your life better?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 4 • I Need To Think</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Isolate the root concern, solve one piece, and re-check readiness.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Of course. Before you go, what specifically would you like to think through — the vehicle, the payment, or timing?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 5 • Payment Objection</span>
    <p><strong>Guest prompt:</strong> “That payment is too high.”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Clarify number vs budget fit, propose two paths, and confirm alignment again.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“I hear you. Let’s solve it cleanly. Is it the monthly number itself, or how it fits your budget priorities?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 6 • Clean Next Steps</span>
    <p><strong>Scenario:</strong> Guest is not buying today.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> End with trust intact: summarize, next step, timeframe, and follow-up promise.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Let me summarize where we landed, what still needs to be clear, and the best next step so this stays easy for you.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Coaching debrief
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Ask After Each Drill</span>
    <ol>
      <li>Did the salesperson recap before numbers?</li>
      <li>Were the numbers presented simply?</li>
      <li>Did the salesperson pause?</li>
      <li>Was the guest’s real concern isolated?</li>
      <li>Was the next step clear?</li>
    </ol>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Watch Points</span>
    <ul>
      <li>Over-explaining</li>
      <li>Filling silence</li>
      <li>Pressure language</li>
      <li>Too many options</li>
      <li>Vague follow-up</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
