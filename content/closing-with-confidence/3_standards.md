# 3.0 Success Standards

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Non-Negotiables</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Simple numbers. Calm pace. Clean commitment ask. Clear next step.</div>
  <p>These standards keep closing from becoming pressure, confusion, or over-talking.</p>
</div>

<div class="quick-grid four">
  <div class="training-card blue"><span class="label-pill">Recap</span><p>Always summarize what matters before numbers.</p></div>

## Eight standards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 1</span>
    <p><strong>Recap before numbers.</strong></p>
    <p>Always summarize what matters most to the guest first.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“From what you told me, the big priorities are safety, comfort, and payment comfort.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 2</span>
    <p><strong>One page, one direction.</strong></p>
    <p>Start with the best-fit path. Do not bounce endlessly between scenarios.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 3</span>
    <p><strong>Calm pace and strategic silence.</strong></p>
    <p>Present. Pause. Let the guest think.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 4</span>
    <p><strong>Confirm before you close.</strong></p>
    <div class="script-line">“Is this matching what you had in mind?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 5</span>
    <p><strong>Use two-option close when needed.</strong></p>
    <p>If uncertain, offer two clean options, not five.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 6</span>
    <p><strong>Separate question, concern, and objection.</strong></p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Question:</strong> clarify information</li>
      <li><strong>Concern:</strong> validate and reassure</li>
      <li><strong>Objection:</strong> isolate, solve, and re-check</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 7</span>
    <p><strong>No pressure language.</strong></p>
    <p>Avoid “If you do it today,” “What do I have to do to earn your business,” guilt, or fear tactics.</p>
    <p>We build urgency through clarity and truth only.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 8</span>
    <p><strong>Clean next steps always.</strong></p>
    <p>Even if they do not buy today: summarize, set a next step, and keep trust intact.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Green / Yellow / Red standard
<div class="quick-grid three">
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Green</span><p>Guest understands numbers, concerns are clear, and commitment or next step is specific.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Yellow</span><p>Guest is interested but numbers, concern, or next action is not fully clarified.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card red"><span class="label-pill">Red</span><p>Guest leaves confused, pressured, or without a specific next step.</p></div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Manager inspection question:</strong> Did the salesperson create clarity and protect trust before asking for commitment?</p>
</div>
