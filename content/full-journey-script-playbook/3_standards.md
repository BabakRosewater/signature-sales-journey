## Communication standards
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">One Clear Intention</span>
    <p>Every stage should have one main job: welcome, discover, support, show, drive, clarify, close, deliver, or follow up.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Short Sentences</span>
    <p>Use short, plain sentences. The guest should not have to decode our process.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">One Decision-Driving Question</span>
    <p>Ask one useful question before moving on.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Confirm Before Recommend</span>
    <p>Confirm what the guest said before offering vehicle, structure, or next-step recommendations.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Process standards
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Follow Stage Order</span>
    <p>Adapt when needed, but do not skip core steps that protect trust: discovery, manager support, trade, test drive, numbers setup, and follow-up.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Explicit Transition</span>
    <div class="script-line">“The next step is...”</div>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Do Not Skip Setup Language</span>
    <p>Trade, test drive, manager introduction, numbers, and delivery need setup language so the guest does not feel surprised.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Defined Next Action</span>
    <p>Every interaction ends with a clear action, owner, and timing.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Green / Yellow / Red standard
<div class="quick-grid three">
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Green</span><p>Script sounds natural, stage is clear, guest understands the next step.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Yellow</span><p>Script is usable but too long, too generic, or missing a transition.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card red"><span class="label-pill">Red</span><p>Script sounds robotic, skips stage setup, or leaves the guest unclear.</p></div>
</div>
