# 5.0 Role-Play Lab: Selection, Obstacles, and Turns

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Practice Reps</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Build comfort and consistency under real-world pressure.</div>
  <p>Use teams of three: sales consultant, guest, and coach/observer. Rotate every 6–8 minutes.</p>
</div>

<div class="score-row">
  <div class="score-card">1 = Unclear</div>
  <div class="score-card">3 = Solid</div>
  <div class="score-card">5 = Natural</div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Score each drill on:</strong> clarity, calm tone, guest autonomy, needs-feature-benefit language, and clear next step.</p>
</div>

---

## Drill cards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 1 • Transition to Selection</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Use discovery recap and 2–3 option framing.</p>
    <p><strong>Must include:</strong> “two or three best-fit options” and a priority confirmation question.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Based on what you told me, I’m going to narrow this to two or three best-fit options so it feels simple.”</div>
    <p><strong>Observer scores:</strong> clarity, calm tone, guest autonomy.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 2 • Primary Walkaround</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Use Needs → Feature → Benefit and invite feedback.</p>
    <p><strong>Must include:</strong> at least three “How does that feel?” check-ins.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Because you mentioned {need}, this vehicle’s {feature} helps by {benefit}. How does that feel for your day-to-day?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 3 • Alternate Selection Pivot</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Read signals and pivot positively without fallback language.</p>
    <p><strong>Guest signal:</strong> “I like it, but I’m not sure about cargo space.”</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Especially since you mentioned {need}, I want to show you another strong fit that still covers the must-haves and adds {extra_benefit}.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 4 • Question / Concern / Objection</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Label the guest’s feedback before responding.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>“Does this have AWD?” = Question</li>
      <li>“I’m worried about monthly payments.” = Concern</li>
      <li>“This is too expensive.” = Objection</li>
    </ul>
    <p><strong>Coach note:</strong> The response should match the feedback type.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 5 • Objection Isolation</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Isolate the real barrier before solving.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Is it more about A or B?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“If we solved X, would you feel comfortable moving forward?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Besides that, is anything else holding you back?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 6 • Turn 100%</span>
    <p><strong>Scenario A:</strong> Guest is stuck on payment and credibility. Consultant turns to manager or finance specialist.</p>
    <p><strong>Scenario B:</strong> You step in cold but must maintain continuity and comfort.</p>
    <p><strong>Observer scores:</strong> turn framed as service, guest did not repeat themselves, next step established.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 7 • Guest Wants to Leave</span>
    <p><strong>Scenario:</strong> Guest says, “I need to think about it.”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Respect the guest while protecting the next step.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“I completely respect that. No pressure at all. If you’d like, I can text or email the top two options we discussed so you can review at your pace.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 8 • Reconnect to Discovery</span>
    <p><strong>Scenario:</strong> Salesperson is feature dumping.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Coach them back to what the guest said matters.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Pause. What did the guest say mattered most, and how does this feature connect to that?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Guest cues to use during drills
<div class="quick-grid three">
  <div class="training-card"><span class="label-pill">Excitement</span><p>“I really like this feature.”</p></div>
  <div class="training-card"><span class="label-pill">Confusion</span><p>“I’m not sure I understand the difference.”</p></div>
  <div class="training-card"><span class="label-pill">Concern</span><p>“I’m worried this may be more than I planned.”</p></div>
</div>

## Debrief questions
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Ask After Each Drill</span>
    <ol>
      <li>What increased trust the most?</li>
      <li>Where did language feel scripted?</li>
      <li>What made the guest feel most in control?</li>
      <li>Did the salesperson link back to discovery?</li>
      <li>Was the next step clear?</li>
    </ol>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Watch Points</span>
    <ul>
      <li>Too many vehicle choices</li>
      <li>Feature dumping</li>
      <li>Arguing with objection</li>
      <li>Turn without context</li>
      <li>Leaving without next step</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
