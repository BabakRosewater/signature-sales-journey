## Objective
Learn how to effectively present vehicle options and address customer questions, concerns, or objections so the guest can make a confident and informed decision.

> “A sale is not something you pursue; it’s what happens to you while you are immersed in serving others.”  
> — Mike White

---

## Why this module matters
This is the point in the journey where discovery becomes visible.

If we did discovery well, selection should feel obvious. The guest should see that we listened and that the options being shown are connected to their life, budget comfort, trade situation, and priorities.

<div class="do-dont">
  <div class="dont-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Weak Selection</span>
    <p>Feature dumping, showing too many vehicles, jumping to price, treating alternate selection like a fallback, or arguing with concerns.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="do-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Strong Selection</span>
    <p>2–3 best-fit options, clear why, needs-feature-benefit language, calm check-ins, and a respectful next step.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Outcomes we are aiming for
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">2–3 Options</span>
    <p>Present a simple set of best-fit choices instead of flooding the guest.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Primary + Alternate</span>
    <p>Use alternate selection as another strong fit, not a lesser option.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Feedback Type</span>
    <p>Separate questions, concerns, and objections before choosing the response.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Empathy + Facts</span>
    <p>Respond with calm language, relevant facts, and a clear next step.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Feel · Felt · Found</span>
    <p>Use the framework naturally, without sounding scripted.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Turn Process</span>
    <p>Give and receive a turn in a way that feels like support, not pressure.</p>
  </div>
</div>
