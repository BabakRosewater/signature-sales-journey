## Part I: Science of selection

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Reciprocity</span>
    <p><strong>What it means:</strong> People naturally respond to helpfulness.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> Small gestures create goodwill and openness.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Can I grab you a coffee or water while we look at the options?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Commitment + Consistency</span>
    <p><strong>What it means:</strong> People act in line with what they already said matters.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> Re-anchor to discovery.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“You said safety is non-negotiable — this trim includes the full safety package.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Social Proof</span>
    <p><strong>What it means:</strong> People look to others when uncertain.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> Normalize the decision with relevant examples.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“This is our most popular choice for families who want comfort and safety.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Authority</span>
    <p><strong>What it means:</strong> Expertise lowers fear.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> Demonstrate knowledge without flexing.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“This model earned strong safety ratings and includes the newest driver-assist technology.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Liking</span>
    <p><strong>What it means:</strong> People trust people they like.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> Make the benefit feel connected to their life.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“If weekend trips are your thing, you’ll love how this cargo area works in real life.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Scarcity</span>
    <p><strong>What it means:</strong> Limited availability can create urgency.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it ethically:</strong> Only when true. Keep it calm and non-pushy.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“This is the last one in this color with the premium package.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Part II: Decision science
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Emotion + Logic</span>
    <p>Guests often decide emotionally and justify logically. Pair story and proof.</p>
    <p><strong>Emotion:</strong> “Picture yourself loading the kids and gear with room to spare.”</p>
    <p><strong>Proof:</strong> “Here is the cargo space, warranty, and safety package.”</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Autonomy</span>
    <p>Guests want choice and control. Present options without flooding them.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“You’ll have choices here — let’s find what fits your lifestyle and priorities.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Fair Comparison</span>
    <p>Bias shapes decisions. Help the guest compare fairly and see long-term value.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Let’s look at total ownership — fuel, warranty, and safety value — not just the sticker.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Perceived Risk</span>
    <p>Most objections are tied to risk: payment, fit, timing, trust, or confidence.</p>
    <p><strong>Coach’s takeaway:</strong> Do not argue with the surface statement. Clarify the real risk.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Part III: Science of the Turn
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Cognitive Dissonance</span>
    <p>If the turn happens without explanation, it can feel awkward. Explain the benefit before it happens.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“To make sure you get the best answer, I’m bringing in our specialist — this will save you time.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Team Support</span>
    <p>Normalize the turn as service, not pressure.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“We’re a team — our job is to make sure you’re fully supported.”</div>
  </div>
</div>
