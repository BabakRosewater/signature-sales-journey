# 4.0 Scripts and Word Tracks

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Word Tracks • Selection + Obstacles</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Use language that connects the vehicle to the guest’s priorities.</div>
  <p>These scripts help the salesperson move from discovery into selection, handle feedback clearly, and use the turn process without pressure.</p>
</div>

<div class="do-dont">
  <div class="dont-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Avoid</span>
    <p>Feature dumping, too many options, arguing with objections, or making the turn feel like a handoff.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="do-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Use</span>
    <p>2–3 best-fit options, needs-feature-benefit language, calm check-ins, objection isolation, and team support.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Selection word tracks
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Discovery → Selection</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Thanks for walking me through what matters most. Based on what you told me, I’m going to narrow this to two or three best-fit options so it feels simple. I’ll explain why each one fits, and you tell me what you want to see first.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Confirm Priorities</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Let me make sure I got this right: your top priorities are {priority_1}, {priority_2}, and {priority_3}. Did I capture that right? Perfect — now I’ll show you a couple of vehicles that match that.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Needs → Feature → Benefit</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Because you mentioned {need}, this vehicle’s {feature} helps by {benefit}. How does that feel for your day-to-day?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Alternate Selection</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Based on what you told me, I have another option I think you’ll really like — especially since you mentioned {need}. This one still covers the must-haves and adds {extra_benefit}.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Feedback word tracks
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Question</span>
    <p class="muted">Clarity + link to need.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Great question. The answer is {answer}. Since you mentioned {need}, that helps because {benefit}. Does that answer it, or do you want me to show you on the vehicle?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Concern</span>
    <p class="muted">Validate → reassure → offer options.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“I understand — that’s a fair concern. Staying within {priority} matters. Here’s what I can do: we can look at {option_1} and {option_2} so it feels comfortable. Would that help?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Objection • AERI</span>
    <p class="muted">Acknowledge, Empathize, Reframe, Invite.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“That’s fair. I understand why that matters — budget is real. Let’s look at what you’re getting for that price, especially around {need}. Would it help if we compare two trims side-by-side and see what changes the payment most?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Feel / Felt / Found</span>
    <div class="script-line">“I understand how you feel. Many guests have felt the same way at first. What they found was that once we looked at the long-term value — {reframe_value} — it became a lot clearer. Would you like to look at that together?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Objection isolation
<div class="quick-grid three">
  <div class="training-card"><span class="label-pill">A or B</span><div class="script-line">“Can I ask a quick question — is it more about {option_a} or {option_b}?”</div></div>
  <div class="training-card"><span class="label-pill">If Solved</span><div class="script-line">“If we solved {barrier}, would you feel comfortable moving forward?”</div></div>
  <div class="training-card"><span class="label-pill">Anything Else</span><div class="script-line">“Besides that, is there anything else holding you back?”</div></div>
</div>

## Turn word tracks
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Giving the Turn</span>
    <div class="script-line">“To make sure you get the best answer and everything feels clear, I’m going to bring in {expert_role}. They’re great at {expertise_area}. I’ll stay right here with you.”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“{team_member_name}, this is {guest_name}. They’re focused on {top_need} and had a question about {topic}.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Receiving the Turn</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Hi {guest_name}, I’m {name}. {salesperson_name} filled me in on what matters most to you, and I’m here to help with {topic}. Let’s keep this simple — what would you like clarity on first?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Guest Wants to Leave</span>
    <div class="script-line">“I completely respect that. No pressure at all. If you’d like, I can text or email the top two options we discussed so you can review at your pace. What’s the easiest way to send that to you?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Manager Coaching Note</span>
    <p>Listen for whether the salesperson ties every solution back to discovery. If not, coach them back to the guest’s original priorities.</p>
  </div>
</div>
