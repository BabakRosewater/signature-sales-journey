## Five principles

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">1 • Psychological Safety</span>
    <p>If the guest feels judged, rushed, or unsafe, they become guarded.</p>
    <p><strong>Safety comes from:</strong> clear expectations, calm pace, permission-based language, and guest control.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“We’ll do a quick loop, about 10–12 minutes, and you tell me what you want to pay attention to most.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">2 • Future Pacing</span>
    <p>People decide faster when they can mentally rehearse ownership.</p>
    <p><strong>Use real scenes:</strong> winter commute, school drop-off, highway passing, parking at work, groceries, weekend road trip.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Picture your normal weekday. Where would this make life easier?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">3 • Feature → Benefit Translation</span>
    <p>Specs can overload the guest. Benefits help them decide.</p>
    <p><strong>Values bridge:</strong> Need → Feature → Feeling → Real-life moment.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Because you mentioned safety, this driver-assist system helps give you peace of mind, especially during highway merging.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">4 • Micro-Confirmations</span>
    <p>People do not always object when they are confused. Sometimes they go quiet.</p>
    <p><strong>Use every 60–90 seconds:</strong></p>
    <div class="script-line">“How does that feel?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Is that what you expected?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Does that solve the thing you mentioned earlier?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">5 • Peak-End Rule</span>
    <p>Guests remember the strongest moment and the ending.</p>
    <p><strong>Plan one peak moment:</strong> quiet cabin, acceleration merge, smooth ride, tech ease, visibility, or parking assist.</p>
    <p>End with calm clarity and a simple next step.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Lens</span>
    <p>The salesperson should not narrate every feature. They should create moments that help the guest feel ownership.</p>
  </div>
</div>
