# 3.0 Success Standards

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Non-Negotiables</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">The test drive must feel safe, relevant, and connected to the guest’s life.</div>
  <p>These standards keep the drive focused on ownership vision instead of feature dumping.</p>
</div>

<div class="quick-grid four">
  <div class="training-card blue"><span class="label-pill">Before</span><p>Set route, time, comfort, priorities, and focus moment.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">During</span><p>Connect features to needs, feelings, and real-life moments.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Check</span><p>Use micro-confirmations every 60–90 seconds.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card dark"><span class="label-pill">After</span><p>Ask fit questions and clarify the next step.</p></div>
</div>

---

## Seven standards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 1</span>
    <p><strong>Pre-drive setup always happens.</strong></p>
    <ul>
      <li>Confirm top two priorities</li>
      <li>Explain route and time</li>
      <li>Invite comfort preferences</li>
      <li>Create one focus moment</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 2</span>
    <p><strong>Use the 70/30 rule.</strong></p>
    <p>Guest experience matters more than consultant performance. The guest should speak and react more than the salesperson lectures.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 3</span>
    <p><strong>Only highlight what matters.</strong></p>
    <p>No feature dumping. Every feature should connect to a stated need, a feeling, and a real-life moment.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 4</span>
    <p><strong>Micro-confirm every 60–90 seconds.</strong></p>
    <div class="script-line">“How does that feel compared to what you expected?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Does that solve the thing you mentioned earlier?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 5</span>
    <p><strong>Use permission-based prompts.</strong></p>
    <div class="script-line">“Would it be helpful if…”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Can I point something out here?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Do you want to feel what this does on the highway?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 6</span>
    <p><strong>Use calm confidence.</strong></p>
    <p>Avoid urgency unless it is true. Clarity is the strongest close.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 7</span>
    <p><strong>Post-drive fit questions always happen.</strong></p>
    <div class="script-line">“How close is this to what you had in mind?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“What did you like most?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“What would you change?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“What do you want to compare it to?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Manager Watch Point</span>
    <p>If the salesperson talks too much, the drive becomes a presentation. Coach them back to questions, silence, and fit checks.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Green / Yellow / Red standard
<div class="quick-grid three">
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Green</span><p>Guest is comfortable, reacting, comparing, and picturing ownership.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Yellow</span><p>Guest is quiet, unclear, or not connecting the vehicle to their life.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card red"><span class="label-pill">Red</span><p>Salesperson feature dumps, ignores silence, or ends without fit questions.</p></div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Manager inspection question:</strong> Did the test drive create an ownership vision, or was it just a ride around the block?</p>
</div>
