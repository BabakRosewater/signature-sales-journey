# 4.0 Scripts and Word Tracks

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Word Tracks • Emotional Test Drive</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Help the guest feel the vehicle fitting their life.</div>
  <p>Use these scripts to set up the drive, create comfort, connect features to feelings, and end with clear fit questions.</p>
</div>

<div class="do-dont">
  <div class="dont-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Avoid</span>
    <p>Feature dumping, over-talking, rushing the route, ignoring silence, or ending the drive without fit questions.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="do-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Use</span>
    <p>Setup, comfort, values bridge, future pacing, micro-confirmations, a peak moment, and a calm post-drive recap.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Pre-drive setup
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">60-Second Setup</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Before we head out, I want to make this easy. We’ll do a quick loop — about 10–12 minutes — so you can feel the ride, the visibility, and how it fits your day-to-day. Before we move, what are the top two things you want to feel or confirm on this drive?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Comfort + Control</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Are you comfortable with the seat and mirrors? Want the music on or quiet so you can hear the cabin?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## During the drive
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Values Bridge</span>
    <p class="muted">Need → Feature → Feeling → Moment</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Because you mentioned {need}, this {feature} helps give you {feeling}, especially when {moment}.”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Because you mentioned winter driving, this AWD system helps give you confidence, especially when roads are slick.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Future Pacing</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Picture your normal weekday. Where would this make life easier?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Micro-Confirmation</span>
    <div class="script-line">“How does that feel compared to what you expected?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Is that what you expected?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Does that solve the thing you mentioned earlier?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Peak Moment Setup</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Do you want to feel what it’s like merging? Nothing aggressive — just enough to see how smooth and confident it feels.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## If the guest seems quiet or unsure
<div class="training-card amber">
  <span class="label-pill">Quiet Guest</span>
  <div class="script-line">“That’s totally okay — this is a lot to take in. What’s going through your mind right now?”</div>
</div>

---

## Post-drive close
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Calm + Clear</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Based on what you told me earlier — {priority 1} and {priority 2} — how close did this feel to what you had in mind?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Fit Questions</span>
    <div class="script-line">“What did you like most?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“What would you change?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“What do you want to compare it to?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“What would make this a yes for you?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Manager coaching note
<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Listen for this:</strong> The salesperson should talk less than the guest, connect features back to discovery, and end with fit questions instead of assuming the drive sold the car.</p>
</div>
