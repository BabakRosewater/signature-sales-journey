# 5.0 Role-Play Lab: Emotional Test Drive

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Practice Reps</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Make the drive feel like an experience, not a presentation.</div>
  <p>Use groups of three: consultant, guest, and observer/coach. Rotate every 8 minutes.</p>
</div>

<div class="score-row">
  <div class="score-card">1 = Feature Tour</div>
  <div class="score-card">3 = Clear</div>
  <div class="score-card">5 = Ownership Vision</div>
</div>

---

## Drill cards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 1 • 60-Second Setup</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Hit all four setup points before leaving the lot.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Route and time</li>
      <li>Top two priorities</li>
      <li>Comfort and control</li>
      <li>Focus moment</li>
    </ul>
    <div class="script-line">“Before we head out, I want to make this easy. We’ll do a quick loop — about 10–12 minutes…”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 2 • Values Bridge Reps</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Translate guest statements into Need → Feature → Feeling → Moment.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>“Safety matters.”</li>
      <li>“I hate road noise.”</li>
      <li>“Winter driving worries me.”</li>
      <li>“I’m on the highway a lot.”</li>
    </ul>
    <div class="script-line">“Because you mentioned {need}, this {feature} helps give you {feeling}, especially when {moment}.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 3 • Micro-Confirmations</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Ask three confirmations without sounding repetitive.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Feel check</li>
      <li>Expectation check</li>
      <li>Fit check</li>
    </ul>
    <div class="script-line">“How does that feel compared to what you expected?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 4 • Quiet Guest Mid-Drive</span>
    <p><strong>Scenario:</strong> Guest becomes quiet and gives short answers.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Name it calmly, ask an open question, and simplify to one focus moment.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“That’s totally okay — this is a lot to take in. What’s going through your mind right now?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 5 • Post-Drive Fit + Next Step</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Ask three fit questions and establish one clear next step without pressure.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Based on what you told me earlier — {priority 1} and {priority 2} — how close did this feel to what you had in mind?”</div>
    <p><strong>Next step options:</strong> compare option / review numbers / alternate drive.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 6 • Peak Moment</span>
    <p><strong>Scenario:</strong> Guest cares about confidence on the highway.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Set up a safe peak moment and connect it to the guest’s concern.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Do you want to feel what it’s like merging? Nothing aggressive — just enough to see how smooth and confident it feels.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Observer scorecard

## Coaching debrief
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Ask After Each Drill</span>
    <ol>
      <li>Did the guest feel safe and in control?</li>
      <li>Did the salesperson talk too much?</li>
      <li>Did features connect to the guest’s life?</li>
      <li>Did silence get handled calmly?</li>
      <li>Was the next step clear?</li>
    </ol>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Watch Points</span>
    <ul>
      <li>Over-talking</li>
      <li>Feature dumping</li>
      <li>No pre-drive setup</li>
      <li>No fit questions after drive</li>
      <li>Ignoring quiet or unsure guest signals</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
