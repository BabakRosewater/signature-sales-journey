## Four principles
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">1 • Familiarity Reduces Anxiety</span>
    <p>People relax when a place feels known. The tour turns “unknown environment” into “known environment.”</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> Keep it short and point out only what helps the guest.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">2 • Social Proof + Credibility</span>
    <p>Seeing a real service operation and real people creates confidence.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> A quick service touchpoint is stronger than a long showroom walk.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">3 • The Support Story</span>
    <p>Guests do not just buy a car. They buy support.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> Connect service and process to the guest’s life.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“This is where you’ll be taken care of.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">4 • Peak-End</span>
    <p>If the tour includes one reassuring moment and ends with clarity, it becomes a positive emotional anchor before numbers.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> One support proof moment and one clear transition sentence.</p>
  </div>
</div>
