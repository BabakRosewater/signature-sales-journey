# 5.0 Role-Play Lab: Dealership Tour & Introduction

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Practice Reps</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Make the tour feel useful, human, and short.</div>
  <p>Use groups of three: consultant, guest, and observer/coach. Rotate every 6–7 minutes.</p>
</div>

<div class="score-row">
  <div class="score-card">1 = Awkward</div>
  <div class="score-card">3 = Clear</div>
  <div class="score-card">5 = Natural Support</div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Score each drill on:</strong> purpose, timing, comfort, service proof, natural language, and clean transition.</p>
</div>

---

## Drill cards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 1 • Permission + Purpose</span>
    <p><strong>Guest prompt:</strong> “Why are we walking around?”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Explain the purpose calmly and ask permission.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“This will take about 3 minutes — can I show you something quick that makes ownership here easier?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 2 • Service Proof Moment</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Explain service support in two sentences and avoid rambling.</p>
    <p><strong>Optional:</strong> include one clean advisor introduction.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“This is our service drive. This is where you’ll check in, and we keep scheduling simple.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 3 • Rushed Guest</span>
    <p><strong>Guest prompt:</strong> “I’m short on time.”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Validate, compress the tour, and transition cleanly.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“No worries — let’s keep this super quick. I just want you to know exactly where support is.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 4 • Tour to Numbers</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Make numbers feel like the natural next step.</p>
    <p><strong>Rule:</strong> No pressure energy. No stalling. Just clarity.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Now that you’ve seen how we support you, let’s sit down and look at the numbers clearly.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 5 • Service Concern</span>
    <p><strong>Guest prompt:</strong> “I’ve had bad service experiences.”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Validate, connect tour to support story, and invite next step.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Totally fair. Service is where trust is earned long-term. That’s why I wanted you to see it — so you know what it’s like here.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 6 • Too Scripted</span>
    <p><strong>Scenario:</strong> Salesperson sounds rehearsed and awkward.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Coach them back to purpose and natural language.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Let’s simplify it. What support do you want the guest to see, and why does it matter to them?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Coaching debrief
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Ask After Each Drill</span>
    <ol>
      <li>Did the tour feel short and useful?</li>
      <li>Did the guest understand why we were doing it?</li>
      <li>Did the service proof moment create confidence?</li>
      <li>Did any language feel corny or scripted?</li>
      <li>Was the transition back to the next step clear?</li>
    </ol>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Watch Points</span>
    <ul>
      <li>Tour too long</li>
      <li>No clear purpose</li>
      <li>Awkward introduction</li>
      <li>Over-talking</li>
      <li>No clean transition</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
