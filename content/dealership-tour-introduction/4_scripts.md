# 4.0 Scripts & Word Tracks

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Word Tracks • Dealership Tour</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Make the tour feel like support, not a detour.</div>
  <p>Use these word tracks to ask permission, explain purpose, create a service proof moment, and transition cleanly to the next step.</p>
</div>

<div class="do-dont">
  <div class="dont-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Avoid</span>
    <p>Long tours, corny language, random introductions, or making the guest feel like you are delaying numbers.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="do-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Use</span>
    <p>Purpose-first language, one support proof moment, quick comfort touchpoint, and a clean transition.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Tour setup
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Permission</span>
    <div class="script-line">“This will take about 3 minutes — can I show you something quick that makes ownership here easier?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Purpose</span>
    <div class="script-line">“I want you to see how we support you after the sale — so it’s not just talk.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Support proof
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Service Credibility</span>
    <div class="script-line">“This is our service drive. This is where you’ll check in, and we keep scheduling simple.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Service Advisor Intro</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Hey {Name}, this is {Guest}. They’re looking at {Model}. I just wanted you two to meet — this is who helps you once you own it.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Comfort Touchpoint</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Waiting area is right here — coffee, Wi-Fi, and restrooms are just around the corner.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Rushed Guest</span>
    <div class="script-line">“No worries — let’s keep this super quick. I just want you to know exactly where support is.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Concerns and transition
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Service Anxiety</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Totally fair. Service is where trust is earned long-term. That’s why I wanted you to see it — so you know what it’s like here.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Transition to Numbers</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Now that you’ve seen how we support you, let’s sit down and look at the numbers clearly.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Manager coaching note
<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Listen for this:</strong> The tour should sound natural, short, and support-focused. If it feels like a script, shorten it and connect it back to ownership confidence.</p>
</div>
