# 3.0 Success Standards

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Non-Negotiables</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Short. Purposeful. Human. Connected to support.</div>
  <p>These standards keep the dealership tour from feeling corny, scripted, or like a stall.</p>
</div>

<div class="quick-grid four">
  <div class="training-card blue"><span class="label-pill">Time</span><p>3–6 minutes max.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Purpose</span><p>Explain why the tour helps the guest.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Proof</span><p>Include one service support moment.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card dark"><span class="label-pill">Transition</span><p>End with a clear next step.</p></div>
</div>

---

## Seven standards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 1</span>
    <p><strong>Short tour: 3–6 minutes.</strong></p>
    <p>Long tours feel like a stall. Keep it purposeful and guest-centered.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 2</span>
    <p><strong>Purpose first.</strong></p>
    <div class="script-line">“I want you to see how we support you after the sale.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 3</span>
    <p><strong>One service proof moment.</strong></p>
    <p>Service drive, advisor introduction, scheduling process, or ownership support. This is the credibility anchor.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 4</span>
    <p><strong>Introductions equal value.</strong></p>
    <p>Only introduce people who add confidence. One to three introductions max.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 5</span>
    <p><strong>No corny scripts.</strong></p>
    <p>Keep it human, calm, and respectful. Avoid “we are one big family” language.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 6</span>
    <p><strong>Clean transition back.</strong></p>
    <div class="script-line">“Now that you’ve seen how we support you, let’s sit down and look at the numbers clearly.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 7</span>
    <p><strong>Guest comfort matters.</strong></p>
    <div class="script-line">“Before we sit down, do you need water or a restroom break?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Manager Watch Point</span>
    <p>If the tour sounds rehearsed, coach it back to purpose, support, and natural language.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Green / Yellow / Red standard
<div class="quick-grid three">
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Green</span><p>Tour is short, useful, support-focused, and transitions cleanly.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Yellow</span><p>Tour is helpful but too long, unclear, or not tied to support.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card red"><span class="label-pill">Red</span><p>Tour feels scripted, corny, awkward, or like a stall before numbers.</p></div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Manager inspection question:</strong> Did the tour increase confidence and make ownership feel easier?</p>
</div>
