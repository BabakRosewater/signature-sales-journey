# 4.0 Scripts & Word Tracks

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Word Tracks • Use Today</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Introduce Josh or Brock by name, purpose, and guest benefit.</div>
  <p>The goal is not to “bring in a manager.” The goal is to make the guest experience feel organized, supported, and useful while the salesperson continues leading the relationship.</p>
</div>

<div class="do-dont">
  <div class="dont-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Avoid</span>
    <p>“Let me get my manager.”</p>
    <p>“At some point early, I’ll introduce you to one of our managers.”</p>
    <p>“My manager wants to make sure we are good listeners.”</p>
    <p>“He is the one who approves everything.”</p>
  </div>
  <div class="do-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Use</span>
    <p>“I want you to meet Josh for a quick second.”</p>
    <p>“He helps lead our guest experience and works with our team on vehicle options, appraisals, and market research.”</p>
    <p>“While we are looking around, he can help check anything in the background that may matter to you.”</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Salesperson word tracks

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Core Daily Version</span>
    <p class="muted">Use once you have enough guest context to make the introduction useful.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“I want you to meet Josh for a quick second. He helps lead our guest experience and works with our team on vehicle options, appraisals, and market research. While we’re looking around, he can help check anything in the background that may matter to you.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Short Version</span>
    <p class="muted">Use when the moment needs to feel quick and natural.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Let me introduce you to Brock real quick. He helps our team with guest experience, vehicle options, and trade values, so he can help check anything in the background while we’re looking.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">With a Trade</span>
    <p class="muted">Use when the guest has a trade or may have a trade.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“I want you to meet Josh real quick. He helps with appraisals and market research. While we’re looking at options, he can start checking the trade side so we’re not waiting on that later.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">New Vehicle / Incoming Inventory</span>
    <p class="muted">Use when availability or incoming units matter.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Let me introduce you to Brock real quick. He helps us with new vehicle availability and incoming inventory, so while we’re looking, he may be able to check a few things in the background for us.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Open to New or Used</span>
    <p class="muted">Use when the customer is open to multiple directions.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“I want you to meet Josh for a quick second. He works with both new and pre-owned options, so while we’re looking around, he can help us make sure we’re seeing the best fit.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Very Casual Version</span>
    <p class="muted">Use when the guest is relaxed and the conversation is moving naturally.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Let me grab Brock for a quick second. He’s really good at helping us make sure we’re not missing a better option based on what you told me.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Josh / Brock word tracks

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard Introduction</span>
    <p class="muted">Enter with a calm hello, support the salesperson, and do not take over.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Nice to meet you. You’re in good hands with _____. I’ll let them keep working with you, but I wanted to introduce myself. I help with our guest experience, vehicle options, and trade values, so if there’s anything we need to check while you’re here, I can help in the background.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">With a Trade</span>
    <p class="muted">Use when appraisal and market research are relevant.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Nice to meet you. I help with our appraisals and market research. While you’re looking at vehicles with _____, I’ll help review the trade side so we can give you clear information when you’re ready.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Comparing Options</span>
    <p class="muted">Use when the guest is unsure between vehicles.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Nice to meet you. I help our team with vehicle options and availability. As _____ learns more about what matters to you, I can help check if there’s anything incoming or available that may fit better.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Short Hello</span>
    <p class="muted">Use when the guest is moving fast or the moment should stay very light.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Nice to meet you. I’ll let _____ keep working with you. I just wanted to put a face with the name in case I can help check anything while you’re here.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## If the guest asks why

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Simple Answer</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Nothing extra you need to do. We just like guests to know who’s helping behind the scenes. If something comes up around availability, trade value, or another option that may fit better, Josh can help us check it quickly.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Time-Sensitive Guest</span>
    <div class="script-line">“I’ll keep it quick. I just want you to know who can help us check inventory, trade, or market information while we keep moving.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Later in the visit

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Before Numbers</span>
    <p class="muted">Use when it is time to bring Josh or Brock back into the conversation.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Before we put everything together, I want to bring Josh back in for a minute. He was helping check the background pieces, and I want to make sure we’re working with the best information.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Before Guest Leaves</span>
    <p class="muted">Use this before the opportunity leaves without clarity.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Before you head out, I want to have Brock say goodbye and make sure we understand the best next step. That way we do not just send you generic follow-up.”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“No pressure — I just want to make sure we understand what would need to be different for this to make sense.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Same-day follow-up

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Josh / Brock Text</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Hi [Name], this is [Josh/Brock] at Clark Hyundai. I appreciated meeting you today. I know the main focus was [vehicle/trade/timing/fit]. I’m going to work with [Salesperson] on the cleanest next option and follow up with you by [time].”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Salesperson Text</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Hi [Name], this is [Salesperson]. Thank you again for spending time with us today. I spoke with [Josh/Brock] after you left, and we are working on the part you said mattered most: [specific concern]. I will follow up at [time] with the next best option.”</div>
  </div>
</div>
