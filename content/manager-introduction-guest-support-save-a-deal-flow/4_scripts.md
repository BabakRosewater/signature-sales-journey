# 4.0 Scripts & Word Tracks

## 4.1 Early salesperson setup
Use this after initial discovery, before vehicle presentation or numbers.

> “Just so you know how we do things here, I’ll help you through the vehicle side, and I’ll introduce you to one of our managers early in the process. We do that because affordability means something different to every guest right now.”

Follow with:

> “Our goal is to make sure we understand what matters most to you — payment, trade, timing, vehicle fit, and overall value — so we can build the right plan around you.”

---

## 4.2 Short daily-use version
Use this when the salesperson needs a simple, repeatable line.

> “At some point early, I’ll introduce you to one of our managers. We do that so we can make sure we are building the right plan around what matters most to you — especially payment, trade, timing, and overall value.”

---

## 4.3 First manager introduction
Salesperson:

> “Before we go too far, I want to quickly introduce you to _____. He’s one of our sales managers. We do this early so you know who is helping behind the scenes and so we can keep everything aligned around what matters most to you.”

---

## 4.4 Manager entry
Manager:

> “I appreciate you coming in. You are in good hands with _____. Before we get too far, I like to ask one simple question: when you think about making this decision, what matters most — payment, trade, vehicle fit, timing, or overall value?”

---

## 4.5 If the guest asks why they need to meet a manager
Salesperson:

> “Fair question. You don’t need to do anything extra. We introduce our managers early so you know who is helping support the process before there is ever a question. It keeps everything simple.”

Manager:

> “My goal is not to slow you down. I just want to make sure we understand the right target before we start putting options together.”

---

## 4.6 Before numbers
Salesperson:

> “Before we put everything together, I want to bring _____ back in for a minute. That way we can make sure the structure matches what you told me matters most.”

Manager:

> “I want to make sure we build this around the right target. Is the biggest focus payment, trade value, money down, timing, or making sure this is the right vehicle?”

---

## 4.7 When the guest is drifting
Salesperson:

> “I want to pause for a second and make sure we are not missing what matters most.”

Manager:

> “What part feels unclear or not comfortable yet?”

---

## 4.8 When the guest says, “We need to think about it”
Manager:

> “Totally fair. When people say that, it is usually one of a few things: the vehicle, the numbers, the trade, the timing, or just needing more confidence. Which one is it for you?”

Follow-up:

> “If we solve that part, are we still in the right conversation?”

---

## 4.9 Before the guest leaves
Salesperson:

> “Before you head out, I want to have _____ say goodbye and make sure we understand the best next step. That way we do not just send you generic follow-up.”

Manager:

> “I do not want to pressure you. I just want to make sure we understand what would need to be different for this to make sense.”

---

## 4.10 Manager same-day follow-up
> “Hi [Name], this is [Manager] at Clark Hyundai. I appreciated you coming in today. I know the main question was [payment/trade/timing/vehicle fit]. I am going to work with [Salesperson] on the cleanest next option and follow up with you by [time].”

---

## 4.11 Salesperson same-day follow-up
> “Hi [Name], this is [Salesperson]. Thank you again for spending time with us today. I spoke with [Manager] after you left, and we are working on the part you said mattered most: [specific concern]. I will follow up at [time] with the next best option.”
