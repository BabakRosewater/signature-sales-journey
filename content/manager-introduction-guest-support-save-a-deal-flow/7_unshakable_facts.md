## Why this belongs in Module 03
Module 03 is where we teach early manager introduction, guest support, showroom flow, affordability alignment, and Save-A-Deal follow-up discipline.

The Unshakable Facts are the evidence behind that process.

They explain why:
- the manager must be aware of active traffic early
- the manager introduction should happen before pressure or friction
- every guest needs a documented next step
- speed-to-lead and speed-to-follow-up are operating standards
- Save-A-Deal is not a last-minute rescue; it is a real-time traffic-control system

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Training point:</strong> These facts are not trivia. They are the operating logic behind how we protect showroom traffic and unsold opportunities.</p>
</div>

## The core facts to teach

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">1 • First Responder Advantage</span>
    <p>Most buyers choose the dealership that responds first.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“This is not just about being good. It is about being first enough to earn the conversation.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">2 • Five-Minute Window</span>
    <p>The highest-quality engagement happens quickly. Waiting turns a live opportunity into a weaker opportunity.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Five minutes is not a target. It is the danger line.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">3 • Qualification Drop</span>
    <p>After the early response window passes, the likelihood of meaningful contact drops sharply.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Same customer. Same vehicle. Different clock. That is why response discipline matters.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">4 • Average Dealer Delay</span>
    <p>If our process is faster, cleaner, and more human, we can win before the average store even responds.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Speed is a competitive advantage only if the message is helpful and the follow-up is disciplined.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">5 • Three-Day Decision Window</span>
    <p>Many buyers make their decision within a short window after engaging.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“The first 72 hours are not casual follow-up time. They are active deal-building time.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">6 • Returning Leads Get Missed</span>
    <p>Returning customers are often overlooked even though they are showing renewed intent.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“When the customer comes back, they are raising their hand again. We cannot miss the second signal.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">7 • Omnichannel Buyer</span>
    <p>Today’s customer moves between online research and in-person buying. The manager touch helps connect the two worlds.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“The manager introduction is the human bridge between what they saw online and what they experience in the store.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">8 • In-Person Still Matters</span>
    <p>Digital tools help, but the showroom experience still decides many deals.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“The customer may start online, but the in-store process still determines confidence.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">9 • Blind Walk-Ins</span>
    <p>A meaningful share of showroom traffic arrives without advance warning, so the team must be ready to pick up the process fast.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Unannounced traffic is why our process must be visible, transferable, and manager-aware.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">10 • Funnel Leaks</span>
    <p>Deals are lost through missed speed, weak handoffs, poor notes, vague follow-up, and no ownership.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Save-A-Deal means we close the leaks before the opportunity disappears.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Manager takeaways
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue"><span class="label-pill">Traffic Awareness</span><p>Know who is in the showroom, where they are in the process, and whether the deal is advancing or drifting.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Early Support</span><p>Introduce manager support early enough to build trust, not late enough to feel like pressure.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Deal Inspection</span><p>Inspect payment, trade, timing, vehicle fit, and decision-maker alignment before the guest leaves.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card dark"><span class="label-pill">Follow-Up Ownership</span><p>Every unsold opportunity needs owner, next action, timing, and CRM notes before the guest exits the lot.</p></div>
</div>

---

## Salesperson takeaways
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue"><span class="label-pill">Do Not Delay</span><p>If a guest is active, follow up while the opportunity is still alive.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Manager Intro</span><p>Manager introduction is not a rescue. It is guest support and alignment.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Document Clearly</span><p>If another person picks up the deal, CRM notes should make the next move obvious.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card dark"><span class="label-pill">Protect the Next Step</span><p>If the guest leaves, the next contact should already be clear, useful, and scheduled.</p></div>
</div>

## Manager inspection questions
<div class="coach-check">
  <ol>
    <li>Did we respond while the opportunity was still warm?</li>
    <li>Did the manager know about the guest early enough to help?</li>
    <li>Did the customer understand who was supporting them?</li>
    <li>Did we identify the real friction: payment, trade, timing, vehicle fit, or decision-maker?</li>
    <li>If unsold, did we leave with owner, next action, timing, and CRM notes?</li>
  </ol>
