# 5.0 Role-Play Lab

## Setup
Use groups of three:
- Salesperson
- Manager
- Guest

Rotate roles every 7 to 8 minutes.

Each drill should be scored on:
- natural language
- guest comfort
- timing
- manager support
- clear next step

---

## Drill 1: Early manager setup
### Scenario
The guest has completed the greeting and early discovery. The salesperson knows the guest is focused on payment comfort and trade value.

### Goal
The salesperson introduces the idea of manager support before there is pressure.

### Required behavior
Salesperson must explain:
- the manager will be introduced early
- the purpose is guest support
- the goal is building the right plan

### Winning line
> “At some point early, I’ll introduce you to one of our managers. We do that so we can make sure we are building the right plan around what matters most to you.”

---

## Drill 2: First manager contact
### Scenario
The salesperson brings the manager in after discovery.

### Goal
The manager enters naturally and does not take over.

### Manager must do three things
1. Support the salesperson.
2. Ask what matters most.
3. Keep the process simple.

### Winning line
> “You are in good hands with _____. I just wanted to meet you early and make sure we are focusing on the right things.”

---

## Drill 3: Guest questions the manager introduction
### Scenario
Guest says:

> “Why do I need to meet a manager?”

### Goal
Salesperson and manager explain the process without sounding defensive.

### Winning line
> “Fair question. We introduce managers early so you know who is helping support the process before there is ever a question.”

---

## Drill 4: Affordability alignment
### Scenario
Guest likes the vehicle but is unsure about payment.

### Goal
Manager identifies what affordability means to the guest.

### Manager must ask
- Is the focus payment?
- Trade value?
- Money down?
- Timing?
- Vehicle fit?
- Overall value?

### Winning line
> “When you think about making this decision, what matters most — payment, trade, vehicle fit, timing, or overall value?”

---

## Drill 5: Busy showroom flow
### Scenario
Three guests are in the showroom:
- one just finished discovery
- one is waiting on numbers
- one is preparing to leave

### Goal
Manager must prioritize the floor.

### Manager must identify
- highest-risk guest
- who needs support now
- whether each guest has met a manager
- what action is needed next

---

## Drill 6: Guest is leaving unsold
### Scenario
Guest says:

> “We need to think about it.”

### Goal
Manager protects the opportunity without pressure.

### Winning line
> “Totally fair. When people say that, it is usually one of a few things: the vehicle, the numbers, the trade, the timing, or just needing more confidence. Which one is it for you?”

---

## Drill 7: Save-A-Deal review
### Scenario
The guest left without buying.

### Goal
Salesperson and manager complete the Save-A-Deal review.

They must answer:
- What happened?
- What stopped the deal?
- What did the guest say exactly?
- What is the next best action?
- Who owns the follow-up?
- When is the follow-up?

---

## Coaching debrief
After each role-play, ask:

1. Did the manager introduction feel natural?
2. Did the salesperson keep control of the relationship?
3. Did the manager add clarity?
4. Did the team identify what mattered most to the guest?
5. Was there a clear next step?
