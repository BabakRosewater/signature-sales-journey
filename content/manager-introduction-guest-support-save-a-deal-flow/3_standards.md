# 3.0 Success Standards

## Standard 1: Manager introduction happens early
The manager should be introduced after early discovery and before the visit reaches pressure, numbers, or exit risk.

The best timing is after the salesperson understands the guest’s priorities.

---

## Standard 2: The manager is positioned as guest support
The manager is not introduced as:
- the closer
- the boss
- the person who approves exceptions
- the person who takes over

The manager is introduced as:
- part of the support team
- someone who helps keep the process clear
- someone who helps align the plan around the guest
- someone who makes sure the guest has a confident path forward

---

## Standard 3: The salesperson sets the expectation
The salesperson should prepare the guest before the manager appears.

Example:

> “At some point early, I’ll introduce you to one of our managers. We do that so we can make sure we are building the right plan around what matters most to you.”

---

## Standard 4: The salesperson stays the hero
The manager should support the salesperson and never make them look weak.

Manager language should include:

> “You are in good hands with _____. I just wanted to meet you early and make sure we are focusing on the right things.”

---

## Standard 5: Affordability is explored before numbers
Before presenting numbers, the team should understand what matters most to the guest.

Common focus areas:
- payment comfort
- trade value
- money down
- timing
- vehicle fit
- overall value
- long-term ownership confidence

---

## Standard 6: Managers control showroom flow
During active traffic, managers must know:
- who is in the showroom
- who is working with each guest
- what vehicle each guest is on
- what stage each guest has reached
- whether the manager has been introduced
- whether the guest is moving forward, drifting, or preparing to leave

---

## Standard 7: Every unsold guest gets a Save-A-Deal review
If a real opportunity leaves unsold, the team must know:
- what happened
- what stopped the deal
- what the guest said exactly
- what the next best action is
- who owns the follow-up
- when follow-up happens

---

## Standard 8: CRM reflects the truth
CRM notes should not be vague.

Avoid:
- “Customer thinking about it.”
- “Will follow up.”
- “Be-back.”

Use:
- real concern
- exact guest language
- next action
- owner
- time
