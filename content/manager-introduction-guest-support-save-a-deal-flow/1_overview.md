# Module 3: Manager Introduction, Guest Support & Save-A-Deal Flow

## Objective
Teach salespeople and managers how to introduce manager involvement early, position the manager as guest support, align around what matters most to the guest, and protect unsold opportunities through Save-A-Deal follow-up.

> “The manager introduction is not a closing move. It is a guest-support move.”

---

## Why this module exists
Salespeople often struggle to introduce managers naturally. They may wait too long, avoid the introduction, or bring the manager in only when the guest has a concern.

When that happens, the manager can feel like a sudden escalation instead of a normal part of the guest experience.

At Clark Hyundai, manager involvement should feel like support, clarity, and alignment.

---

## Where this fits
This module belongs early in the guest visit.

It comes after:
- Greeting & First Impressions
- Uncovering Needs & Building Trust

It comes before:
- Showcasing Solutions & Overcoming Obstacles
- Emotional Test Drive
- Decision Point & Building Value
- Closing with Confidence
- Manager Closes

Once the salesperson has uncovered what matters to the guest, it is the right time to normalize manager support.

---

## The core shift
Old mindset:

> “I get the manager when I need help.”

New mindset:

> “I introduce the manager early so the guest knows they have a team helping them make a confident decision.”

---

## What this module builds
This module builds five disciplines:

1. **Natural manager introduction** — the salesperson introduces the manager early and confidently.
2. **Guest support positioning** — the manager is introduced as support, not escalation.
3. **Affordability alignment** — the team identifies what matters most: payment, trade, money down, timing, vehicle fit, and overall value.
4. **Showroom flow awareness** — managers know who is in the showroom, where each guest is in the process, and where support is needed.
5. **Save-A-Deal follow-up discipline** — every unsold opportunity has a real reason, next best action, owner, and follow-up time.

---

## What great looks like
A guest should think:

> “This dealership is organized, clear, and helping me make a smart decision.”

A salesperson should think:

> “My manager supports me and strengthens the process.”

A manager should think:

> “I am entering with context and helping align the guest’s priorities.”

---

## The operating principle
Manager involvement must be normalized before it is needed.

If the first manager contact happens only after the guest has a concern, we waited too long.

---

## The one-line standard
Every real showroom opportunity should have:

**Manager introduction → Guest support alignment → Clear next step → Save-A-Deal follow-up if unsold**
