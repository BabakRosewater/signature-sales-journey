# 2.0 Science: Why Early Manager Introduction Works

## The guest responds to timing and tone
The same manager can feel helpful or uncomfortable depending on when and how they are introduced.

If the manager appears only after the guest raises a concern, the guest may feel like the process changed.

If the manager is introduced early, the guest experiences the manager as part of the dealership’s normal support process.

---

## Early introduction reduces surprise
Surprise creates resistance.

When the salesperson says early that a manager will be introduced, the guest has context. Later manager involvement feels expected, not sudden.

This is why the manager introduction should happen after early discovery, not at the end of the visit.

---

## Affordability is personal
In today’s market, affordability means something different to every guest.

For one guest, affordability may mean the lowest payment.

For another, it may mean:
- stronger trade value
- less money down
- shorter term
- lease flexibility
- fuel economy
- warranty protection
- long-term ownership confidence

The early manager introduction creates a chance to identify what affordability means to that guest before numbers are presented.

---

## The salesperson keeps credibility
A strong manager introduction should not make the salesperson look weak.

The salesperson is not giving up control. The salesperson is adding support.

The manager should strengthen the salesperson by saying:

> “You are in good hands with _____. I just wanted to meet you early and make sure we are focusing on the right things.”

---

## The manager enters with context
When the salesperson introduces the manager properly, the manager enters with useful context.

The manager can ask better questions:
- What matters most about the decision?
- Is the biggest focus payment, trade, timing, vehicle fit, or overall value?
- What would make this feel like a responsible decision?

This allows the manager to support the visit before friction builds.

---

## Save-A-Deal starts before the guest leaves
Save-A-Deal is not only a follow-up tool after the guest is gone.

It starts during the visit by identifying:
- what the guest wants
- what still feels unclear
- what may stop the decision
- what next step would keep momentum alive

The better the manager introduction, the better the Save-A-Deal follow-up becomes.
