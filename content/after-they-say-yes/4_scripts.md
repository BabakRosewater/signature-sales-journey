# 4.0 Scripts & Word Tracks

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Word Tracks • After Yes</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Protect the yes with calm clarity and predictable next steps.</div>
  <p>Use these word tracks to confirm the commitment, explain the roadmap, set expectations, hand off cleanly, and reduce anxiety.</p>
</div>

<div class="do-dont">
  <div class="dont-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Avoid</span>
    <p>Sudden tone shifts, disappearing after commitment, vague timelines, or making finance feel like pressure.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="do-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Use</span>
    <p>Clear roadmap, update promises, warm handoffs, reassurance, and predictable communication.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Confirm + celebrate
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Confirm the Yes</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Perfect. So we’re going with the {vehicle} in {trim/color}. Sound good?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Celebrate Briefly</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Nice choice — it really matches what you told me matters most.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Three-step roadmap
<div class="training-card amber">
  <span class="label-pill">Roadmap</span>
  <div class="script-line">“Here’s what happens next — it’s simple: 1) We’ll confirm a few details. 2) We’ll get paperwork moving with our finance team. 3) Then we’ll set up delivery and go over features so it’s easy from day one.”</div>
</div>

---

## Time expectations and reassurance
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Time Expectation</span>
    <div class="script-line">“This usually takes about {X} minutes. I’ll keep you updated so you’re never wondering what’s happening.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">No Surprises</span>
    <div class="script-line">“My goal is zero surprises. If anything changes, I’ll tell you right away and explain it clearly.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Warm handoff and finance transition
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Warm Handoff to Finance</span>
    <div class="script-line">“{Name} is going to help with the paperwork and options that protect your ownership experience. They’re very straightforward — their job is to make this clean and easy.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Permission-Based Transition</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Would you like me to quickly explain what they’ll cover in there so it feels predictable?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Anxiety and waiting moments
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Guest Gets Nervous</span>
    <div class="script-line">“That makes sense. A lot of people feel that way after making a big decision. We’re in a good spot — I’ll keep it simple and keep you informed.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Waiting Update</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Quick update for you — we’re waiting on one last step and should have you moving again in about {X} minutes. I’ll check back in shortly.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Future pacing
<div class="training-card green">
  <span class="label-pill">Positive Future Pacing</span>
  <div class="script-line">“Picture the first week with it — what’s the first place you’re driving?”</div>
</div>

---

## Team language
<div class="training-card blue">
  <span class="label-pill">We’re on Your Side</span>
  <div class="script-line">“We’re on your team — we’re just finishing the steps that make ownership smooth.”</div>
</div>

---

## Manager coaching note
<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Listen for this:</strong> The guest should feel guided, not processed. The after-yes phase should feel organized, warm, and predictable.</p>
</div>
