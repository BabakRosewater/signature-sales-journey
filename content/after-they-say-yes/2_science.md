## Five principles

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">1 • Buyer’s Remorse Risk Peaks After Commitment</span>
    <p>Once the decision is made, the brain looks for confirmation that it was a good choice.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> confirm the choice, reinforce fit in one sentence, then move into the roadmap.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">2 • Uncertainty Feels Like Risk</span>
    <p>Small unknowns feel larger after yes: timing, handoff, trade steps, paperwork, and delivery.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> give a short three-step sequence and honest time expectation.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">3 • Tone Shift Breaks Trust</span>
    <p>Guests notice if the energy changes from warm to transactional.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> keep the same calm, caring tone after commitment.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">4 • Peak-End Matters</span>
    <p>The ending becomes the memory that drives CSI, reviews, referrals, and loyalty.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> create a small positive peak and a confident ending.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">5 • Cognitive Load Kills Momentum</span>
    <p>Too many details create fatigue and impatience.</p>
    <p><strong>Use it:</strong> three steps, one sentence per step, and confirm understanding.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Lens</span>
    <p>The guest does not need every detail. They need to know the process is under control.</p>
  </div>
</div>
