# 5.0 Role-Play Lab: After They Say Yes

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Practice Reps</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Practice protecting the guest’s confidence after commitment.</div>
  <p>Use groups of three: consultant, guest, and observer/coach. Rotate every 8 minutes.</p>
</div>

<div class="score-row">
  <div class="score-card">1 = Confusing</div>
  <div class="score-card">3 = Clear</div>
  <div class="score-card">5 = Confidence-Building</div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Score each drill on:</strong> clarity, warmth, roadmap, time expectation, handoff, update discipline, and guest confidence.</p>
</div>

---

## Drill cards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 1 • Confirm + Celebrate</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Make the yes clear and positive without hype.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Perfect. So we’re going with the {vehicle} in {trim/color}. Sound good?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Nice choice — it really matches what you told me matters most.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 2 • Three-Step Roadmap</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Deliver the roadmap smoothly and confidently.</p>
    <p><strong>Must include:</strong> three steps, time expectation, and update promise.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Here’s what happens next — it’s simple...”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 3 • Warm Handoff</span>
    <p><strong>Guest says:</strong> “So now I have to go into finance?”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Reframe as support, explain with permission, and introduce finance as a benefit.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Would you like me to quickly explain what they’ll cover in there so it feels predictable?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 4 • Anxiety Spike</span>
    <p><strong>Guest says:</strong> “I don’t know… I’m feeling nervous now.”</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Name emotion, reassure, reinforce fit, and return to roadmap.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“That makes sense. A lot of people feel that way after making a big decision. We’re in a good spot — I’ll keep it simple and keep you informed.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 5 • Waiting Room Update</span>
    <p><strong>Scenario:</strong> Guest has waited 12 minutes.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Give a short update, new time estimate, and calm reassurance.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Quick update for you — we’re waiting on one last step and should have you moving again in about {X} minutes. I’ll check back in shortly.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 6 • No Surprises</span>
    <p><strong>Scenario:</strong> A detail may change or approval timing is not final.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Keep trust by explaining calmly and early.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“My goal is zero surprises. If anything changes, I’ll tell you right away and explain it clearly.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Coaching debrief
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Ask After Each Drill</span>
    <ol>
      <li>Did the guest know exactly what happens next?</li>
      <li>Did the tone stay warm after commitment?</li>
      <li>Was finance positioned as support?</li>
      <li>Was the time expectation clear?</li>
      <li>Was anxiety reduced or increased?</li>
    </ol>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Watch Points</span>
    <ul>
      <li>Energy drop after yes</li>
      <li>Vague time expectation</li>
      <li>No update promise</li>
      <li>Cold handoff to finance</li>
      <li>Guest waiting without communication</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
