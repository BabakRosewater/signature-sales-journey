# 5.0 Role-Play Lab: Manager Close

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Practice Reps</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Practice a manager close that feels like clarity, not pressure.</div>
  <p>Use groups of three: sales consultant, manager, and guest. Rotate every 7–8 minutes.</p>
</div>

<div class="score-row">
  <div class="score-card">1 = Intense</div>
  <div class="score-card">3 = Clear</div>
  <div class="score-card">5 = Supportive</div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Score each drill on:</strong> clean handoff, calm tone, category diagnosis, Unshakable Facts, one ask, and handoff back to consultant.</p>
</div>

---

## Drill cards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 1 • Clean Handoff</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Consultant sets up the manager turn without “uh-oh” energy.</p>
    <p><strong>Manager goal:</strong> Enter calmly and support the consultant.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“My manager is here to make sure we are taking great care of you and keeping this simple.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 2 • Diagnose the Category</span>
    <p><strong>Guest prompts:</strong></p>
    <ul>
      <li>“I just need to think.”</li>
      <li>“I saw one online cheaper.”</li>
      <li>“I am not ready to commit.”</li>
      <li>“I do not understand the numbers.”</li>
    </ul>
    <div class="script-line">“Help me understand — are you looking for more information, or is there something that does not feel comfortable yet?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 3 • Unshakable Facts</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Deliver three facts calmly, then one recommended path.</p>
    <p><strong>Rule:</strong> No debate. No intensity. Just clarity.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Here are the unshakable facts so this stays simple...”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 4 • Two Options + One Ask</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Offer two options max and end with one clean ask.</p>
    <p><strong>Time limit:</strong> 90 seconds.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Based on what matters most, we really have two clean options...”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 5 • Consultant Stays the Hero</span>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Manager supports the consultant and hands the guest back cleanly.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“_____ will take great care of the next step and keep this simple.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Drill 6 • Unsold Guest Protection</span>
    <p><strong>Scenario:</strong> Guest still leaves without buying.</p>
    <p><strong>Goal:</strong> Capture real reason, next best action, owner, and follow-up time.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“Before you leave, I want to make sure we are clear on the next best step.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

## Coaching debrief
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Ask After Each Drill</span>
    <ol>
      <li>Did the handoff feel clean?</li>
      <li>Did the manager lower tension?</li>
      <li>Was the real issue diagnosed?</li>
      <li>Were the facts used kindly?</li>
      <li>Was there one clear ask or next step?</li>
    </ol>
  </div>
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Coach’s Watch Points</span>
    <ul>
      <li>Manager taking over</li>
      <li>Undermining consultant</li>
      <li>Adding too many options</li>
      <li>Arguing with guest</li>
      <li>Vague follow-up if unsold</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
