# 3.0 Success Standards

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Non-Negotiables</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Support the guest, protect the salesperson, clarify the truth, and ask once.</div>
  <p>These standards keep the manager close from feeling like a takeover.</p>
</div>

<div class="quick-grid four">
  <div class="training-card blue"><span class="label-pill">Handoff</span><p>No surprise turn. The manager enters with context.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Tone</span><p>Calm pace, low ego, no interruption.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Facts</span><p>Unshakable Facts create clarity.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card dark"><span class="label-pill">Ask</span><p>One clean question and one next step.</p></div>
</div>

## Seven standards

<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 1</span>
    <p><strong>Clean handoff. No surprise turn.</strong></p>
    <p>Sales consultant sets up the manager as support.</p>
    <div class="script-line">“My manager is here to make sure we are taking great care of you and keeping this simple.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 2</span>
    <p><strong>Positioning and tone matter.</strong></p>
    <ul>
      <li>Sit at an angle, not across the table like a confrontation.</li>
      <li>Use calm pace and low ego.</li>
      <li>Do not interrupt or take over.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 3</span>
    <p><strong>Diagnose the category before solving.</strong></p>
    <ul>
      <li>Question = needs information</li>
      <li>Concern = needs reassurance or clarity</li>
      <li>Objection = blocking belief</li>
      <li>Misalignment = vehicle, expectations, or structure are not aligned</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 4</span>
    <p><strong>Use Unshakable Facts kindly.</strong></p>
    <div class="script-line">“Here is what is true so we can make the best decision.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 5</span>
    <p><strong>Two options max.</strong></p>
    <p>Option A is the best fit. Option B is the simple alternative.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 6</span>
    <p><strong>One ask and one next step.</strong></p>
    <div class="script-line">“If we can do X, are you comfortable moving forward today?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card red">
    <span class="label-pill">Standard 7</span>
    <p><strong>Never weaken your consultant.</strong></p>
    <p>Avoid blaming, correcting, or making the salesperson look unprepared. The consultant stays the hero.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Manager Watch Point</span>
    <p>If the guest becomes more guarded after the manager enters, the manager probably added intensity instead of clarity.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Green / Yellow / Red standard
<div class="quick-grid three">
  <div class="training-card green"><span class="label-pill">Green</span><p>Manager lowers tension, clarifies the real issue, and creates a clean next step.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card amber"><span class="label-pill">Yellow</span><p>Manager helps but does not fully isolate the real issue or next action.</p></div>
  <div class="training-card red"><span class="label-pill">Red</span><p>Manager surprises the guest, takes over, adds intensity, or weakens the salesperson.</p></div>
</div>

<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Manager inspection question:</strong> Did I make this feel simpler and safer for the guest?</p>
</div>
