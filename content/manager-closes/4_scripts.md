# 4.0 Scripts & Word Tracks

<div class="training-hero">
  <div class="eyebrow">Word Tracks • Manager Close</div>
  <div class="bigIdea">Use calm manager language that clarifies the real issue and protects trust.</div>
  <p>These word tracks help managers enter cleanly, diagnose accurately, use facts kindly, simplify options, and ask once.</p>
</div>

<div class="do-dont">
  <div class="dont-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Avoid</span>
    <p>Surprise entry, taking over, correcting the salesperson, debating, discount chasing, or multiple asks.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="do-box">
    <span class="label-pill">Use</span>
    <p>Supportive entry, category diagnosis, Unshakable Facts, two options max, one ask, and a clean handoff.</p>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Manager entry and diagnosis
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Trust-First Introduction</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Hi, I’m _____. I just wanted to stop in and make sure we are taking great care of you. Before we go further, what is the most important thing for you to feel good about today?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Category Finder</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Help me understand — are you looking for more information, or is there something that does not feel comfortable yet?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“What part feels unclear or heavy right now?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Clarity and options
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">Unshakable Facts</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Here are the unshakable facts so this stays simple: 1) ________. 2) ________. 3) ________. Given that, our cleanest path is ________.”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Two-Option Simplifier</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Based on what matters most, we really have two clean options: Option A is ________ and Option B is ________. Which one feels closer to what you described?”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Common manager close moments
<div class="quick-grid two">
  <div class="training-card green">
    <span class="label-pill">Price / Payment Pressure Release</span>
    <div class="script-line">“That makes sense. Nobody wants to feel stretched. If we can structure it in a way that feels responsible, would you feel comfortable moving forward?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card amber">
    <span class="label-pill">What Would Make This a Yes?</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Fair question — what would need to be true for this to feel like a yes today?”</div>
    <div class="script-line">“Okay, so if we solve that, are we good?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card dark">
    <span class="label-pill">Respectful Close</span>
    <div class="script-line">“If we can do ________, are you comfortable taking the next step with us today?”</div>
  </div>

  <div class="training-card blue">
    <span class="label-pill">Handoff Back to Consultant</span>
    <div class="script-line">“Perfect. _____ will take great care of the next step and keep this simple.”</div>
  </div>
</div>

---

## Save-A-Deal if unsold
<div class="training-card red">
  <span class="label-pill">Unsold Guest Protection</span>
  <div class="script-line">“Before you leave, I want to make sure we are clear on the next best step. What would need to be different for this to make sense?”</div>
</div>

---

## Manager coaching note
<div class="coach-check">
  <p><strong>Listen for this:</strong> The manager should make the situation feel simpler. If the guest gets more guarded, the manager is probably adding intensity instead of clarity.</p>
</div>
